Preparing for Influenza Season - Consecutive Analysis

OVERVIEW

This staffing optimization analysis project aims to analyze staffing data to address peak season staff shortages and regional personnel misallocation. The focus is on identifying the main factors causing personnel shortages and surpluses across different periods to optimize staffing strategies effectively.

PROCESSING

1. Cleaning data to ensure high quality and consistency.

2. Integrating datasets from diverse sources to gain a comprehensive view.

3. Testing a hypothesis using the integrated data to derive meaningful insights.

4. Analyzing correlations to identify relationships between variables.

5. Creating visualizations for spatial and temporal data representation.

6. Formulating actionable conclusions and recommendations based on the analysis outcomes.

7. Tailoring analysis components to meet the specific needs of the intended audience.

RECOMMRNDATIONS

1. Aligning healthcare worker planning with state mortality rather than population size, with specific emphasis on California and Florida.

2. Addressing February influenza peaks by augmenting staffing levels during this period.

3. Implementing the preselection of specialized staff for vulnerable age groups, particularly focusing on the 1-4 children's age group and the 65+ age group.

4. Prioritizing the preselection of health workers who have received flu shots for the upcoming influenza season to minimize staffing imbalances.

5. Prospective collaboration involving the analysis of staffing efficiency within the current and historical periods.
